A little bit about me...

Hi I'm Elwyn! I'm a second generation Filipino-American, pun enthusiast, avid gamer, boba lover, and an aspiring software engineer with interests in machine learning and game development.

I discovered a passion for computer science during my time in college. Coming in as a mechanical engineering major, I had no real exposure to computer science until I took an introductory course at UCLA. From there, I fell in love with the problem solving process behind computer science and that wonderous feeling that comes with finishing a new project. My goal is to be able to share that feeling with others, to create things that'll leave them with feelings of amazement, excitement, and happiness.

Here on my website I'll be posting updates to any of my projects, hobbies, and life in general. Take a look at some of the other tabs if you have time!

PROJECTS

Blueprint

November 2020 - July 2021

During the course of the school year, I've been working with the UCLA organization Blueprint to develop a web application using React for a non-profit that helps connect farmers to other non-profits or individuals to help address food insecurity in the Los Angeles area. My contributions have been in creating the Inventory Manager section of the app! In it, sellers can create, edit, and delete their listings, as well as privatize or publicize them with the click of a button! Additionally, they can also sort or filter listings based on certain attributes of their listings, like the expiration date, price, produce type, etc.

MTGDraftBot

March 2020

I made a Discord bot in Python in order to have an online Magic: The Gathering draft with some friends! A common problem we had was that other online services would crash if a player disconnected, ruining the draft, or it would make exporting decklists really difficult. To remedy this, I created our own DraftBot! It runs the draft on Discord so any player disconnecting just needs to reconnect to Discord to resume the draft! If they are disconnected for too long, it times out and automatically picks a card for them. At the end of the draft, it generates a simple .txt file that can be used to import the decklist into Cockatrice, a platform for playing Magic: The Gathering online.

ANT

June 2020 - December 2020

I worked on the ANT as a research assistant for SMERC at UCLA. The ANT is an autonomous last mile delivery robot for larger packages. This project is defintely one of the most interesting and educational projects I've worked on. It's taught me to use softwares and languages like ROS and Swift, and since it is powered by a Linux computer, I've gotten more comfortable using terminal and the command line on Linux.

Scene Recognition

October 2019
Scene Recognition is a project I completed for my Computer Vision course. In it, I developed and trained a machine learning model using SIFT, SURF, and ORB feature detection in order to classify pictures of rooms with around 70% accuracy. The accuracy for each of these methods of feature detection were compared to discover the most effective one.

ZombieDash

February 2019
ZombieDash is a real time game I implemented for my Data Structures and Algorithms class. In the game, the player rescues citizens from zombies by leading them towards an exit. The entire project was done using C++. Some interesting aspects were the design of weapons and other tools for the player to fight off and survive the hordes of zombies. Overall, this project was a really fun way to learn to use data structures to make a real game.

"I can't dance . . . "

Previous Next

At least, that's what I'd always thought. But when I came to UCLA for an overnight orientation, Bruin Life Weekend, one of the first things I saw was a competitive dance team: Samahang Modern. They were a subset of Samahang Pilipino, a cultural organization for Filipinos like me. And they were really amazing. I'd always wanted to be able to dance, but I'd never been very musically inclined, so I always figured that I just couldn't.

But then I found a beginner-friendly dance organization - Foundations. I joined one of their training teams and honestly it was both horrifying and amazing. It was scary and awkward at first, diving into a group of 30 or so people and dancing in front of them when I'd never even felt comfortable dancing by myself. But I got to meet so many people, and have so many unforgettable experiences, that I would do all that awkwardness over again. So dance truly has given me so much more than just some movements to music. It's given me a community of supportive friends. It's given me a new creative outlet, a way to express myself in ways words can't. It's given me strength to face and overcome my fears. Dance has really changed me for the better.

Since then, I joined a competitive dance team, VSU Modern, performed my own choreos in showcases, and competed in multiple competitions! Although my competing days are over for now, I still attend some dance classes and freestyle with my partner when I have time.
